Tax Strategy
TCG Reseller — Sole Proprietor
Goal
Maintain an honest, consistent, and documented record of TCG business activity. The IRS does not require perfection — it requires a reasonable methodology applied consistently year over year. This app is that methodology.
Revenue — High Confidence
All Whatnot sales are captured exactly from the WN Earnings CSV — hammer price, platform fees, and net payout per transaction. Non-Whatnot sales are imported from Collector or entered manually. Revenue figures should be treated as complete and accurate.
Cost of Goods — Tiered Approach
Tier 1 — High Value (>$50)
Tracked individually in Collector with exact purchase price. Imported directly — cost basis is precise. These should represent the majority of total COGS by dollar value.
Tier 2 — Low Value (≤$50)
Not always tracked individually. When no exact record exists, cost basis is estimated using the your configured fill % of sale price — adjustable in the ⟷ Fill tab before auto-filling on bulk/mid-range cards. This is applied consistently across all unrecorded low-value sales.
Tier 3 — Unaccounted Spend
Total purchases (from bank/Whatnot orders) minus recorded COGS = unaccounted spend. This represents inventory not yet sold, losses on bad pulls, or wheels that yielded unsellable cards. The portion attributable to cards still in inventory will reconcile at year-end. The remainder is treated as a cost of doing business — wheels and gambling on pulls are a legitimate reseller expense when cards are acquired with intent to resell.
The Spend Gap — What It Means
Spend Gap = Total Purchases − Recorded COGS − Current Inventory Value
A positive gap is expected and normal. It typically represents:
① Cards still sitting in inventory (will close as they sell) ② Losses on pulls/wheels — paid for cards that weren't worth what we expected ③ Small purchases not yet recorded — will estimate cost basis if/when sold A gap that grows faster than inventory grows is a sign of net losses on speculative buying. That's a real business insight, not an accounting error.
Year-End Process
1. Export Whatnot Earnings for the full year → import into Sells
2. Export Whatnot Orders for the full year → import into Buys
3. Export bank CSV (Rocket Money) → import, skip WN charges already in tracker
4. Export Collector → import for inventory snapshot and high-value COGS
5. Run Auto-fill costs on ≤$50 WN sales with no cost basis
6. Review ⊗ Dupes on both Buys and Sells tabs, bulk-delete
7. Open Tax Report tab → review Schedule C figures
8. Note the Spend Gap — document what you believe it represents
9. Save a snapshot export for your records

🚀 Getting Started ▼
1
Import your inventory
Go to Import → Smart Drop and drag in any CSV export from Collector or Whatnot. The app auto-detects the file type. Start with your Collector inventory export if you have one.
2
Or add cards manually
Go to Inventory and tap + Add Card. Fill in the card name, game, cost paid, and market value. You can always update market values later.
3
Check Market Intelligence
Go to Market and tap Refresh Prices. The app fetches current market data and shows you which cards have SELL signals based on your target margin.
4
Back up your data
Tap Backup in the top-right corner regularly. Your data lives in your browser — an export is your safety net. Import it back anytime to restore.
📥 Import Guide ▼
Smart Drop is the easiest way — just drop any supported CSV and it detects the type automatically.
📋
Collector Inventory CSV
From Collector app: Portfolio → Export → CSV. Contains your cards, cost basis, and market values. This is your main inventory import.
🛒

From Whatnot seller dashboard: Orders → Export. Contains cards you bought. Each row gets marked as Business, Personal, or Lot.

🔗 Cost Basis Matching ▼
When you sell a card on Whatnot, the earnings CSV doesn't include what you paid for it. The app tries to figure out your cost basis automatically so your P&L is accurate.
How does the app find my cost basis?
It uses a 3-step waterfall:

1. Match to Buys — Compares the sold card name against your buy records using word overlap scoring. If a buy matches with 60%+ word overlap, that purchase price is used. A 90%+ overlap is marked high confidence; 60–89% is medium.

2. Match to Inventory — If no buy matches, the same name-matching runs against your Collector inventory. Cards imported from Collector usually have an exact cost basis from the app.

3. Fill % Estimate — For sales under $150 with no name match, the app estimates cost as a percentage of the sale price. The default is 45% but you can adjust this in the Sells → Auto-fill view to match your actual average margin. High-value cards with no match are flagged for manual entry.
What is the Fill % setting?
Your fallback cost estimate. If you typically pay 40% of what you sell cards for, set it to 40%. The app shows your actual average from recorded data and lets you use that with one tap. Only applies to low-value WN sales with no matched buy or inventory record.
How do I improve matching accuracy?
Fill in card names on your Whatnot earnings during import — the slot title is shown as a placeholder. The more names you provide, the better the app can match sells to buys. Importing your Collector inventory also helps since those have precise card names and cost data.
🔄 Collector ↔ Whatnot Sales ▼
If you sell on Whatnot and also track sales in the Collector app, you'll have two records for the same sale. The app detects and handles these duplicates automatically.
How does duplicate detection work?
When you import Collector sales, the app compares each one against your existing Whatnot Earnings sells. A match is found when:

• Sale price is within $1 (fees may cause small differences between platforms)
• Date is within 10 days of each other

Matched Collector rows are auto-marked as Skip during import since the Whatnot Earnings record is authoritative — it has the exact fee breakdown, order ID, and payout amount.
Which record wins — Collector or Whatnot?
Whatnot Earnings is authoritative. It comes directly from the platform with exact commission, processing fees, and net payout. Collector sales are useful for card names and cost data, but the Whatnot record is what your taxes should be based on. Duplicate Collector rows are flagged with a link to the matching WN record.
What about Whatnot Orders vs Bank transactions?
The same idea applies to buys. If you import both a Whatnot Orders CSV and a bank statement, the app cross-matches them by exact amount (±2¢) and date (±5 days). The WN Orders row is preferred since it has the seller name and card details. Bank rows are enriched with that info or flagged as duplicates.
📊 Market Intelligence ▼
What do the signals mean?
SELL — Market price meets or exceeds your target margin. Good time to list.
BUY — Card appears undervalued vs recent market data.
HOLD — Price is below your margin target. Wait for appreciation.
TRADE — Cards you've manually flagged for trading.
How is the target sell price calculated?
Target = Cost Basis ÷ (1 - margin%). Fee toggles (Whatnot, processing, shipping) are added on top to ensure your net after fees still hits the margin. The formula preview on the Market page shows the exact math.
Where does market data come from?
Prices are fetched via AI web search when you tap Refresh Prices. The AI finds recent sold listings and current listings from TCGPlayer, eBay, and other marketplaces. Confidence is shown per card (high/mid/low dot).
AI features require the API key to be configured server-side. Toggle AI on via the ⚙ Settings menu to enable price refresh and market analysis.
🔒 Your Data ▼
All data is stored locally in your browser's localStorage. Nothing is sent to any server except when you use AI features (card names + prices sent to Claude API).
What if I clear my browser data?
Your TCG Tracker data will be deleted. Always export a backup before clearing browser data or switching devices. Use Backup in the top-right menu.
How do I move to a new device?
Export a backup JSON from the old device, then open TCG Tracker on the new device and import that file via Settings → Import Backup.
Can I use this offline?
Yes — once loaded, the app works fully offline. AI price refresh requires internet. Install it as a PWA (Add to Home Screen) for the best offline experience.
📝 Schedule C & Taxes ▼
This is not tax advice. The Schedule C page is a tool to help you organize your data — consult a tax professional for your specific situation.
What does the Schedule C page show?
A pre-filled approximation of IRS Schedule C (Profit or Loss from Business) using your sells, cost of goods, and expenses. It exports to a PDF you can share with your accountant.
What counts as a business expense?
Shipping supplies, grading fees, marketplace subscriptions, storage, photography equipment used for TCG. Mark personal purchases as "Personal" during import so they're excluded.
